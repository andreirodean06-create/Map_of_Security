# Open Problem: Post-Quantum Migration

## Context

Many current public-key systems, such as RSA and elliptic-curve cryptography, could be broken by a powerful enough quantum computer. The problem is not only inventing safer algorithms. It is also replacing old systems across browsers, servers, banks, governments, devices, and archives without breaking everything.

## The Beginner Version

Imagine a city where every lock must be replaced, but people still need to enter buildings every day. Security teams must choose new locks, test them, update instructions, and avoid leaving old keys around.

## Why It Is Hard

* Old systems may be hidden in legacy software.
* Some devices are difficult or impossible to update.
* New algorithms may need larger keys or signatures.
* Organizations must protect data that needs to remain secret for many years.

## Starter Questions

* Where does an application use TLS, certificates, or digital signatures?
* What data would still be sensitive in 10 or 20 years?
* Which systems can be updated easily, and which cannot?
