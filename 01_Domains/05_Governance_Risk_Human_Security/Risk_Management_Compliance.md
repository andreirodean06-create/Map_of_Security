# Risk Management & Compliance

> The formal quantification of technical vulnerabilities, compilation of asset threat registries, design of operational controls, and administrative auditing against global security standards.

## Main Activities

### Dimension | Focus Areas | Action Items
* **Theory:** Risk optimization frameworks, compliance standards, mitigation accountability matrices, technical asset valuation metrics.
* **Experiment:** Generating enterprise risk registers, scoring hypothetical breach scenarios, testing controls, organizing multi-department tabletop exercises.
* **Design:** Assigning permanent risk ownership, drafting formal exception documentation, building technical remediation roadmaps.

## Connections

* **Guides:** [[Network_Infrastructure_Security_Domain]] and [[UVT_CS_Security_Landscape]] by dictating the network access policies, password lengths, and compliance baselines required for student and enterprise infrastructure.
* **Receives evidence from:** [[SOC_Monitoring_Detection]] and [[Incident_Response_Forensics]] to dynamically adjust risk scores based on real-world log anomalies.
* **Influences:** [[Security_Architecture_Program_Management]] by shaping strategic focus areas around verified organizational exposure zones.

## Important Problems

* **The Priority Vacuum:** Flaws where internal asset logs categorize every single warning as "High Risk," creating systemic alert blindness and rendering threat prioritization useless.
* **Paper-Shield Compliance:** Passing formal external audits and check-box verification scripts while leaving severe, unpatched logical flaws open to exploit.
* **Orphaned Asset Accountability:** Insecure organizational environments where systemic risks are identified, but no business owner or technical admin accepts the responsibility to fix them.
* **The Translation Deficit:** The open problem of mapping low-level, hyper-technical software findings into macro-economic risk data that corporate leadership can immediately act upon.
