# Security Awareness & Social Engineering

> The behavioral analysis, cognitive threat mapping, systemic usability optimization, and structural simulation of human-centric manipulation vectors within technical authentication boundaries.

## Main Activities

### Dimension | Focus Areas | Action Items
* **Theory:** Human decision psychology, adversarial persuasion mechanics, phishing taxonomy models, usable engineering principles, defensive workplace incentives.
* **Experiment:** Dissecting weaponized email payloads, executing controlled phishing simulation routines, surveying corporate notification processing speed.
* **Design:** Constructing adaptive internal training loops, engineering explicit one-click reporting pipelines, hardening user authentication interfaces.

## Connections

* **Supports:** [[SOC_Monitoring_Detection]] by transforming individual human alerts into live indicators of wide-scale phishing distributions.
* **Guides:** [[Web_API_Security]] account recovery interfaces, dictating the step-by-step logic and identity checks behind multi-factor bypass workflows.
* **Influences:** [[Security_Architecture_Program_Management]] by profiling real-world human error trends to shape wider technological fallback rules.

## Important Problems

* **Cognitive Phishing Exploits:** The ease with which advanced adversaries craft highly targeted spear-phishing structures to steal primary credentials or bypass multi-factor push notifications.
* **Warning Fatigue Overload:** Systemic patterns where users see repetitive, non-actionable browser alerts so frequently that they instinctively click "Accept" without thinking.
* **Brittle Recovery Lifecycles:** Vulnerable out-of-band validation pipelines where social engineering actors trick customer support agents into changing account emails over the phone.
* **The Real-World Usability Threshold:** The open problem of creating defensive network protocols that everyday users can realistically maintain without finding dangerous, insecure workarounds.
